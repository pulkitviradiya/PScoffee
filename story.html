Why "P.S."?

We didn't start with coffee.
We started with a question.

Why does specialty coffee cost ₹320 in India? The beans are Indian. The baristas are Indian. The margin isn't.

Why "P.S."?

The afterthought that turned out to matter most.

P.S. — The line you add when the letter is already done. The thought that refused to stay quiet.

A postscript is what you write after you think the letter is done. It's the thought that refused to stay quiet. The line that turns out to be the most important one in the whole message.

We think great coffee works the same way. It's not on the calendar. It's the cup you grabbed on the way to your desk that changed how the next three hours felt. Not because it was grand — because it was right.

P.S. Coffee was built to be that for people who get their mornings done in co-working spaces, corporate lobbies, and university corridors. The daily habit that earns its place, quietly, cup after cup.

What we believe

Specialty coffee has been asking the wrong people to pay for it.

P.S. — The best cup of the day should be for Tuesday. Not just weekends.

"The working professional. The student. The freelancer. They're being priced out of their own ritual."

The ₹300+ café is real. The quality is real. But the people who actually drink coffee every day — the working professional, the student, the freelancer at a co-working desk — they're being priced out of their own ritual.

We believe the best cup of the day should be accessible to the person who actually needs it most. Not a weekend treat. A Tuesday habit. That's the entire brief — and we've never moved off it.

Specialty coffee in India has a gatekeeping problem that looks like pricing. We're fixing the pricing.

How we built it

100% Arabica. No shortcuts. The numbers still work.

P.S. = Plain & Simple. Smarter model. Same bean.

We didn't find a cheaper bean to hit the ₹89 price point. We found a smarter model.

Small-format decks. Co-working and campus locations where the rent is a fraction of a street-facing café. A focused menu of drinks people actually order. Supply sourced directly from estates in Chikmagalur and Coorg — Indian Arabica, properly traceable, at honest prices.

The result: specialty grade coffee, at the price of an ordinary cup. We made the numbers work without touching the bean. That's the whole trick. And it turns out it wasn't that hard — once you stop trying to build a café experience and start trying to build a habit.
