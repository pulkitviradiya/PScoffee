P.S. — The Founding Idea · No. 06

An instant coffee brand without the instant coffee.

100% espresso shot from 100% genuine specialty beans — ready before you arrive. Built from a single honest observation about who actually drinks coffee every day.

01 — The observation

The people who drink coffee every day are being asked to pay for the people who drink it once a week.

The co-working professional who needs a 9am cappuccino. The gym member grabbing a cold brew before a 6am session. The student nursing a latte through a three-hour deadline. The freelancer who orders twice before noon without thinking about it. These people are the real coffee market.

For years, the industry has been building infrastructure for the wrong customer. The specialty cafe exists to create an experience. We exist to make the experience irrelevant — not by lowering quality, but by removing everything that inflates the price without improving the cup.

02 — The founding equation

We lowered our rent. Not our beans.

No real estate premium. No interior design budget. No table service. No ambiance tax. We go where the drinker already is, fit inside the building they're already in, and hand them a specialty-grade cup in under 90 seconds.

We did not lower the ingredient standard to hit the price point. We lowered everything else. That's the entire model — stated plainly, because the cup is the only thing worth performing.

03 — Why "P.S."

The postscript
psychology.

A postscript is the thought that refused to stay quiet. The last line that turns out to be the most important one.

P.S. Coffee is that moment — the cup you almost skipped, the one that changed how the morning felt. We're not trying to be the headline. We're trying to be the P.S.: the thing people realise they cannot do without.

The cup lines are written as postscripts. The app notifications end with P.S. The subscription is the P.S. Pack. The cup — grabbed at the Pod, held while walking to the desk — is designed to feel like a quiet, essential addendum to whatever comes next.

05 — The anti-pretense contract

We lowered our overheads, not our standards. You get the coffee. We keep the pretense.

Indian specialty coffee has conflated quality with theatre — the pour-over ritual, the single-origin origin story, the barista's explanation of the tasting notes. These have value in the right context. But for a person who needs a cappuccino before a 9am meeting, the theatre is the cover charge for the drink.

We are exactly what we say we are. Specialty grade. Grab-and-go. Honest price. The only theatre we allow is the quality itself — and that theatre plays out in the cup, not at the counter.
