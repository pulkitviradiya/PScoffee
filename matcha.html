01 — What ceremonial grade means

The matcha most places serve is a culinary powder built to survive sugar and ice. Ours is the grade you'd whisk at a tea ceremony — just made at the speed you actually live.

Ceremonial grade is the first spring harvest — the youngest, shade-grown leaves, stone-milled into a powder so fine it suspends in water without a fight. It's vivid green, naturally sweet, and built to be tasted on its own, not buried under syrup.

We treat it that way. Sifted, measured, whisked to a proper foam — not shaken from a bottle. The convenience is in the Pod and the app. The matcha itself gets no shortcuts.
